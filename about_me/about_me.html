About me

I am

I am a second-year student at Georgia Institute of Technology pursuing a B.S. in Computer Engineering with concentrations in Signal and Information Processing & Computing Hardware and Emerging Architectures and a minor in International Affairs. I am from Peachtree Corners, GA and have lived in Georgia my entire life. As I love both hardware and software development, I have chosen to major in computer engineering as the path I have chosen is an even mix of electrical engineering and computer science. My interests lie in digital signal processing algorithm development, DSP hardware development, post-Moore computing (neuromorphic hardware, quantum computing), data analytics, and back-end software development.

Outside of school, my hobbies include tinkering with electronics and... learning. When I was a child, nothing brought me more joy than taking apart electronics and rebuilding, reconfiguring, or amalgamating them, but now that I've grown and have experience in electronic design, I have started creating my own devices rather than taking apart something prebuilt. Moreover, I'd like to think of myself as a life-long learner; simply put, there is nothing I enjoy more than learning. Fortunately, these hobbies work harmoniously; if I do not know what I am doing while creating a new electronic device, I am able to gain the satisfaction of learning something new to complete the project!
